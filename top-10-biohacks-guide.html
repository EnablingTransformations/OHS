The Top 10 Biohacks for Longevity, Energy & Peak Performance

Science-backed protocols from the world's leading longevity and biohacking research. No fluff, no product pitches — just the knowledge you need to start optimizing today.

This guide distills the most impactful, evidence-backed biohacks from the longevity and human optimization field. These aren't fringe theories — they're the foundational protocols that physicians, researchers, and elite performers consistently rely on.

The order matters. We've ranked these from most foundational (start here) to more advanced. Master the basics before chasing the exotic — that's the #1 lesson from every serious biohacker.

1

Optimize Your Sleep Architecture

The single most powerful biohack that costs nothing

Sleep is not rest — it's active repair. During deep sleep, your body releases growth hormone, clears metabolic waste from the brain via the glymphatic system, consolidates memory, and repairs damaged DNA. Poor sleep is directly linked to accelerated biological aging, insulin resistance, weakened immunity, and cognitive decline.

Protocol

  • Temperature: Keep your bedroom at 65–68°F (18–20°C). Core body temperature must drop for sleep onset.
  • Light discipline: Eliminate all blue/white light 60–90 minutes before bed. Use red or amber lighting in the evening. Block all LEDs in the bedroom.
  • Morning sunlight: Get 10+ minutes of direct sunlight within 30 minutes of waking. This sets your circadian clock and improves sleep quality 14–16 hours later.
  • Consistent timing: Same wake time every day (including weekends), within a 30-minute window.
  • Last meal: Finish eating 3+ hours before bed. Late meals elevate core temperature and blood glucose, suppressing deep sleep.
  • Supplements (optional): Magnesium L-Threonate or Glycinate (200–400mg), Apigenin (50mg), and L-Theanine (100–200mg) taken 30–60 min before bed.
Why it matters: VO₂ max and sleep quality are the two strongest predictors of all-cause mortality. Fix sleep first — everything else works better when you do.
2

Structured Exercise: The Longevity Drug

The single most studied intervention for extending healthspan

No supplement, drug, or device comes close to the impact of consistent, structured exercise on longevity. Your muscles produce myokines — hormone-like signaling molecules that act as the body's own pharmacy.

The Minimum Effective Dose

  • Zone 2 cardio: 150–180 min/week (brisk walking, easy cycling, swimming at conversational pace). Builds mitochondrial density and fat oxidation.
  • VO₂ max training: 1–2 sessions/week of high-intensity intervals (4×4 min at 85–95% max heart rate). VO₂ max is one of the strongest predictors of lifespan.
  • Strength training: 2–3 sessions/week targeting all major muscle groups. Muscle mass protects against metabolic disease and cognitive decline.
  • Flexibility & balance: Daily mobility work (10–15 min). Prevents injury and maintains functional independence.
The data: Moving from the bottom 25% to above-average fitness reduces all-cause mortality risk by roughly 50%. Exercise is the closest thing we have to a longevity drug.
3

Nutrition: Eat for Cellular Health

What you eat determines how fast — or slow — you age

The evidence consistently points to a few principles that span every credible longevity researcher: eat mostly whole, unprocessed foods; prioritize protein for muscle preservation; minimize sugar, seed oils, and ultra-processed food.

Protocol

  • Protein priority: 1.0–1.2g per pound of lean body mass daily, spread across meals. Non-negotiable for muscle preservation after age 30.
  • Eliminate ultra-processed food: If it has ingredients you can't pronounce, minimize it. Drives inflammation, insulin resistance, and gut dysbiosis.
  • Eat the rainbow: 30+ different plant foods per week. Polyphenols, fiber, and micronutrients feed your microbiome and reduce oxidative stress.
  • Healthy fats: Extra virgin olive oil (2 tbsp/day), wild-caught fatty fish (2–3x/week), avocados, and nuts.
  • Time-restricted eating: 10–12 hour eating window. Supports autophagy and circadian rhythm alignment.
Key insight: The best diet is one you can sustain, built on whole foods, adequate protein, and minimal processed junk. Consistency beats perfection.
4

Cold & Heat Exposure

Hormetic stress: what doesn't kill your cells makes them stronger

Deliberate cold and heat exposure activate stress-response pathways — cold shock proteins, heat shock proteins, norepinephrine, and AMPK — that improve resilience, reduce inflammation, and support cardiovascular health.

Cold Exposure

  • Target: 11 minutes total per week spread across 2–4 sessions.
  • Temperature: 45–60°F (7–15°C). Cold enough that you want to get out but can safely stay in.
  • Benefits: Increases norepinephrine 200–300%, improves dopamine baseline, reduces inflammation, enhances brown fat thermogenesis.

Sauna / Heat Exposure

  • Target: 2–4 sessions/week, 15–20 minutes at 176–212°F (80–100°C).
  • Benefits: Activates heat shock proteins, improves cardiovascular function, supports detoxification through sweat.
The research: A 20-year Finnish study found that sauna use 4–7 times/week was associated with a 40% lower risk of all-cause mortality compared to once-per-week use.

Stress Management & Nervous System Regulation

Chronic stress accelerates aging at the cellular level

Chronic stress shortens telomeres, increases cortisol, drives systemic inflammation, disrupts sleep, and accelerates every marker of biological aging.

Protocol

  • Physiological sigh: Two quick inhales through the nose + one long exhale through the mouth. Fastest parasympathetic activation in real-time.
  • Daily breathwork: 5–10 min of box breathing (4 sec in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold). Reduces baseline cortisol and improves HRV.
  • Nature exposure: 20+ minutes outdoors reduces cortisol by 20–25%.
  • Social connection: Loneliness is as dangerous as smoking 15 cigarettes a day. Relationships are a longevity intervention.
  • Gratitude practice: 3 things daily rewires neural pathways, reducing anxiety and improving sleep.

Optimize Your Gut Microbiome

Your gut is your second brain — and your immune system's headquarters

70–80% of your immune system resides in your gut. The microbiome influences mood (90% of serotonin is gut-produced), inflammation, nutrient absorption, and gene expression.

Protocol

  • Fiber diversity: 30+ different plant foods per week feeds different beneficial bacterial species.
  • Fermented foods: 1–2 servings daily: sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, yogurt, kombucha. More effective than probiotic supplements for microbiome diversity.
  • Eliminate gut disruptors: Minimize artificial sweeteners, emulsifiers, excessive alcohol, unnecessary antibiotics, and NSAIDs.
  • Bone broth or collagen: Supports gut lining integrity through glycine, proline, and glutamine.
  • Test, don't guess: Comprehensive stool analysis or microbiome test to identify imbalances.
Stanford study: A diet high in fermented foods increased microbiome diversity and decreased 19 inflammatory markers over just 10 weeks.

Red & Near-Infrared Light Therapy

Photobiomodulation: using light to power your mitochondria

Red light (630–670nm) and near-infrared (810–850nm) penetrate tissue to stimulate mitochondrial function, increase ATP production, reduce inflammation, and accelerate repair. Backed by thousands of peer-reviewed studies.

Protocol

  • Duration: 10–20 min per session, 6–12 inches from a quality panel.
  • Skin health: Stimulates collagen production, reduces wrinkles, accelerates wound healing.
  • Recovery: Near-infrared supports muscle recovery, joint health, and inflammation reduction.
  • Brain health: Transcranial photobiomodulation is being studied for cognitive enhancement and neuroprotection.
  • Hair growth: Red light at 655nm for 6+ min/day shows significant results in clinical trials.

The Essential Supplement Stack

Fill the gaps that even a perfect diet can't cover

Foundational Stack

  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): 2–3g daily. Target an Omega-3 Index of 8–12%. Supports cardiovascular, brain, and joint health.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2: 2,000–5,000 IU D3 daily (target 40–60 ng/mL). K2 (MK-7, 100–200mcg) directs calcium into bones, not arteries.
  • Magnesium: 200–400mg daily. L-Threonate for brain, Glycinate for sleep, Malate for energy.
  • Creatine: 5g daily. Supports brain function, muscle preservation, and cellular energy. One of the most studied supplements.

Advanced Longevity Stack (With Medical Guidance)

  • NAD+ precursors (NMN or NR): 500mg–1g daily. Restores declining NAD+ levels critical for DNA repair and mitochondrial function.
  • Resveratrol: 500mg–1g daily with fat. Activates sirtuins, mimics caloric restriction benefits.
  • Berberine: 500mg 1–2x daily. Activates AMPK, supports blood glucose control. Often compared to metformin.
  • Quercetin + Fisetin: Senolytic compounds that clear damaged cells. Cycled 2–3 days/month, not daily.
Important: Always test before supplementing. A comprehensive blood panel gives you a baseline so you can measure what works for YOUR biology.

Track Your Biomarkers

You can't improve what you don't measure

Key Biomarkers

  • Blood work (2–4x/year): Fasting glucose & insulin, HbA1c, lipid panel (ApoB, Lp(a)), hsCRP, homocysteine, ferritin, thyroid panel, hormones (testosterone/estrogen/DHEA), Vitamin D, Omega-3 Index.
  • Biological age: Epigenetic clocks (TruAge, GrimAge) measure pace of aging. Test annually.
  • Wearables: Track sleep staging, HRV, resting heart rate, and recovery trends daily.
  • Body composition: DEXA scan annually for visceral fat, muscle mass, and bone density.
  • Inflammation: ESR and hsCRP — the silent drivers of age-related disease.
The principle: Measure → Intervene → Re-measure → Adjust. This is the core loop of biohacking. Without it, you're guessing with expensive supplements.

Peptides & Hyperbaric Oxygen

Advanced therapies at the cutting edge of longevity science

Once you've mastered the foundations, these advanced modalities represent the frontier of biological optimization. Explore with medical guidance.

Peptides

Short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules for recovery, growth hormone optimization, gut healing, and cognitive enhancement.

Key Peptides

  • BPC-157: Accelerates tissue repair — tendons, ligaments, muscle, gut lining. Available orally and via injection.
  • CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin: Stimulates natural growth hormone release. Supports recovery, body composition, and sleep quality.
  • GHK-Cu: Supports skin regeneration, collagen synthesis, and has anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Epithalon: Telomerase activator studied for slowing cellular aging.

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)

Delivers oxygen at higher-than-atmospheric pressure, enhancing mitochondrial efficiency, stimulating new blood vessel formation, and accelerating healing.

HBOT Protocol

  • Frequency: 3–5 sessions/week therapeutic; 1–2 for maintenance.
  • Duration: 60–90 minutes at 1.3–2.0 ATA.
  • Benefits: Enhanced recovery, improved cognition, reduced inflammation, accelerated wound healing.
Important: Peptides and HBOT should be explored under the guidance of a knowledgeable physician. Quality, dosage, and individual biology all matter.
