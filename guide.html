Top 10 Biohacks from the World's Leading Longevity Experts

The science-backed protocols endorsed by the biggest names in biohacking and longevity — distilled into 10 actionable steps you can start today.

Curated from the protocols of

Bryan Johnson · Blueprint Andrew Huberman · Huberman Lab Peter Attia · Outlive Gary Brecka · Ultimate Human Dave Asprey · Bulletproof David Sinclair · Lifespan Ben Greenfield

These aren't fringe theories or expensive treatments. They're the foundational habits that the world's top longevity experts actually practice every single day. Each one is backed by research, costs little or nothing, and can be started immediately.

1

Morning Sunlight Exposure (Within 30 Minutes of Waking)

Endorsed by: Huberman · Brecka · Johnson · Asprey

Get direct sunlight in your eyes within 30 minutes of waking — no sunglasses, no windows. This triggers a cortisol pulse that sets your circadian clock, boosts alertness, and starts a 14–16 hour countdown to natural melatonin release at night. Huberman calls this "the single most important thing you can do for your sleep."

Start Today:Step outside for 5–10 minutes on a sunny morning, 10–20 minutes on a cloudy day. Don't stare at the sun — just face the direction of the light. Do this before looking at your phone.
2

Deliberate Cold Exposure

Endorsed by: Huberman · Asprey · Greenfield · Brecka

Cold showers or cold plunges (1–3 minutes at 45–60°F) cause a sustained dopamine increase of up to 250% above baseline — lasting for hours without a crash. This also spikes norepinephrine, which sharpens focus and mood. Unlike caffeine, this is a clean, long-lasting energy boost.

Start Today:End your morning shower with 30–60 seconds of the coldest water you can handle. Work up to 2–3 minutes over a few weeks. Do it after exercise, not before.
3

Prioritize Sleep Above Everything

Endorsed by: Johnson · Attia · Huberman · Asprey · Greenfield

Every expert on this list ranks sleep as the #1 health lever. Bryan Johnson goes to bed at 8:30 PM. Peter Attia dedicates an entire section of Outlive to it. Huberman calls it "the most important nootropic, stress reliever, immune booster, and emotional stabilizer." Poor sleep accelerates biological aging faster than almost any other factor.

Start Today:Fix your wake time (same time every day, including weekends). Dim lights after 8 PM. Aim for 7–8 hours. Consider Huberman's sleep stack: magnesium L-threonate (145mg), L-theanine (200mg), and apigenin (50mg), 30–60 minutes before bed.
4

Zone 2 Cardio (The Longevity Sweet Spot)

Endorsed by: Attia · Greenfield · Johnson · Sinclair

Peter Attia calls exercise "the most powerful longevity drug" — and Zone 2 cardio (where you can hold a conversation but it's slightly uncomfortable) is the foundation. It builds mitochondrial density, improves fat oxidation, and is one of the strongest predictors of lifespan. Attia recommends 150–180 minutes per week.

Start Today:Walk briskly, cycle, or swim for 30 minutes, 4–5 times per week, at an effort level where you could talk but would rather not. Use a heart rate monitor to stay in Zone 2 (roughly 60–70% of max heart rate).
5

Delay Caffeine 90–120 Minutes After Waking

Endorsed by: Huberman · Asprey

When you wake up, adenosine (the molecule that makes you sleepy) is still clearing from your system. Drinking caffeine immediately blocks this natural clearing process, leading to an afternoon crash. By waiting 90–120 minutes, you let cortisol do its job first — then caffeine amplifies your natural alertness instead of masking tiredness.

Start Today:Set a timer when you wake up. Drink water with a pinch of sea salt first. Get sunlight, move your body, and only then have your coffee. You'll notice steadier energy all day.
6

30 Grams of Protein Within 30 Minutes of Waking

Endorsed by: Brecka · Attia · Greenfield

Gary Brecka's viral "30/30/30" rule: eat 30 grams of protein within 30 minutes of waking, then do 30 minutes of low-intensity movement. Protein first thing stabilizes blood sugar, supports muscle maintenance, and reduces cravings for the rest of the day. Peter Attia emphasizes protein as the most important macronutrient for longevity.

Start Today:Have eggs (3–4 eggs = ~24g protein), a protein shake, or Greek yogurt within 30 minutes of waking. Follow with a 30-minute walk. Track your protein intake for a week — most people are severely under-consuming.
7

Track Your Biomarkers (Measure, Don't Guess)

Endorsed by: Johnson · Attia · Brecka · Sinclair

Bryan Johnson's core philosophy is "measure, intervene, measure again." You can't optimize what you don't track. Peter Attia recommends comprehensive blood panels annually at minimum. Gary Brecka's 10X Health System starts with genetic testing. Even basic markers — fasting glucose, lipid panel, CRP, vitamin D, hormones — reveal what's actually happening inside your body.

Start Today:Schedule a comprehensive blood panel with your doctor. At minimum, ask for: fasting glucose, HbA1c, complete lipid panel, CRP (inflammation), vitamin D, testosterone/estrogen, thyroid panel, and a complete metabolic panel.
8

Extra Virgin Olive Oil (2 Tablespoons Daily)

Endorsed by: Johnson · Asprey · Sinclair · Greenfield

High-polyphenol extra virgin olive oil is one of the few foods that virtually every longevity expert agrees on. Bryan Johnson takes 2 tablespoons daily as a foundational longevity food. The polyphenols reduce inflammation, support cardiovascular health, and activate sirtuins — the same longevity pathways that David Sinclair studies at Harvard.

Start Today:Get a high-quality, high-polyphenol EVOO (look for harvest date and polyphenol count on the label). Take 1–2 tablespoons daily — on salad, with food, or straight. Avoid cooking at high heat with it.
9

Strength Training (Protect Muscle Mass for Your "Marginal Decade")

Endorsed by: Attia · Greenfield · Johnson · Huberman

Peter Attia's concept of the "marginal decade" — the last decade of your life — is defined by whether you maintained muscle mass and bone density in your 30s, 40s, and 50s. Muscle is the organ of longevity: it regulates blood sugar, produces myokines (anti-inflammatory molecules), and prevents falls — the leading cause of death in older adults.

Start Today:If you don't already strength train, start with 2–3 sessions per week. Focus on compound movements: squats, deadlifts, rows, presses, and carries. Even bodyweight exercises count. The goal is progressive overload over years, not intensity in weeks.
10

Stop Eating 3+ Hours Before Bed

Endorsed by: Johnson · Huberman · Asprey · Sinclair

Eating close to bedtime disrupts sleep quality, raises resting heart rate, impairs overnight cellular repair, and spikes blood glucose at the worst possible time. Bryan Johnson stops eating by 11 AM. Huberman recommends finishing dinner at least 3 hours before sleep. Dave Asprey calls late-night eating "the most common biohacking mistake."

Start Today:Set a "kitchen closes" time — at least 3 hours before you plan to sleep. If you sleep at 10 PM, stop eating by 7 PM. Track how your sleep quality changes after just one week of this habit.
